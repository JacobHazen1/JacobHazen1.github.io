A brief self-introduction: I like machine learning, data science and programming—this is because I go insane if my mind is not occupied.

I am a Economics Ph.D. candidate at McGill University. My research interest involves the overlap of Economics, Data Science, and Computer Science—specifically Natural Language Processing and its applications to Macroeconomics.

Apart from being a student, I enjoy most of my time being outdoors and off my computer. In the winter, I am an frequent hockey player, casual snowboarder, and runner. During the warmer months, I am a frequent skateboarder.
